## Best Practices

1. **Value-Based Pricing**: Price based on value delivered, not cost
2. **Anchoring Effect**: Show high price first to make others seem affordable
3. **Decoy Effect**: Strategic middle tier drives more premium tier adoption
4. **Willingness-to-Pay Research**: Conduct surveys to understand customer value perception
5. **Psychological Pricing**: Use $X9 pricing, avoid round numbers
6. **Annual Discounts**: 15-20% discounts drive annual adoption and reduce churn
7. **Feature Bundling**: Don't unbundle; bundle value to justify higher pricing
8. **Usage-Based Add-ons**: Let power users pay for advanced features
9. **Clear Comparison**: Make tier differences crystal clear (not confusing)
10. **Test Relentlessly**: Price optimization never ends; test quarterly

## Pricing Models

- **Freemium**: Free tier + paid upgrades (best for viral growth)
- **Tiered**: Starter/Pro/Enterprise (most common, high conversion)
- **Usage-Based**: Pay-per-use (fair for variable demand)
- **Hybrid**: Base fee + usage overage (combines benefits)
- **Seat-Based**: Price per user (standard for team software)
- **Value-Based**: Price tied to business outcome (premium positioning)
